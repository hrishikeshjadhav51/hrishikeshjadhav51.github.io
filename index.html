About

Hrishikesh is a Ph.D. scholar at IIT Bombay, specializing in advanced materials and manufacturing research. He graduated as a Gold Medalist from NIT Arunachal Pradesh (M.Tech, 2018) in Manufacturing Engineering. During his master’s program, he developed a novel simulation model for successive single-spark EDM micro-machining, contributing to the understanding of non-conventional machining processes.

He is currently working on a SERB-funded research project titled “On Self-Organisation of Titania Nanotube Arrays via Anodization: Influence of Substrate Microstructure, Surface Lay, and Form.” His research focuses on titania nanotube (TiNT) growth mechanisms, particularly on complex metal substrates and freeform surfaces. His expertise spans advanced characterization techniques including FIB-SEM, EDS, XRD, CV, EIS, and nanoindentation, along with a strong foundation in electrochemistry, corrosion science, and precision micromachining.

Beyond research, Hrishikesh brings the same intensity to his personal pursuits. He is a martial artist and nunchaku practitioner, a pianist with a creative edge, and an enthusiastic mixologist. A passionate traveler, he has explored much of India, with a special fondness for Northeast Indian cuisine. Whether it’s crafting cocktails or preparing barbeques by a riverside, he enjoys blending science, art, and adventure into every experience.

Research Interests

His research interests include TNT growth investigation on metal alloys and freeform surfaces and advanced metallurgical characterization that includes FIB-SEM, EDS, XRD, CV, EIS, and Nanoindentation. Non-conventional micromachining is another area of his interest that focuses on manufacturing components with micron tolerances. He has interests in an interdisciplinary study that involves the study of electrochemistry, corrosion science, mechatronics, material characterization, and advanced imaging and spectroscopy techniques.
